Workshops

Join us for one of the full-day workshops on Wednesday, August 27.

  • Ana Tudor
  • Axel Rauschmayer
  • Dan Rubin
  • Denise Jacobs
  • Peter-Paul Koch

Ana Tudor – «Breaking Out of the Rectangle»

View on Eventbrite

For a long time, everything on the web has been just flat rectangular boxes. However, this has changed and now you can let you imagination roam free in 3D. You can stretch and rotate things, place them on a circle, on a cylinder or on a sphere and you can even fold or roll things in order to create enticing interfaces that just pull people in.

Covering from the core concepts behind to browser inconsistencies and performance considerations, this workshop is going to help you master transforms at an advanced level even if you've never even dipped your toes into this part of CSS before.

Axel Rauschmayer – «JavaScript in Depth»

View on Eventbrite

JavaScript has grown up and its popularity has expanded far beyond web browsers – to servers, mobile and desktop applications, databases, robots and more. It is therefore increasingly important to know this language well.

This workshop is for people who are already programming JavaScript and would like to get acquainted more deeply with it. I will cover the history and background of JavaScript, power features (property attributes, integers, etc.), lesser-known pitfalls (Unicode, array indices, etc.) and coding patterns (IIFE, etc.).

We won’t go into browser-related topics (rendering performance, etc.), but I will give an introduction to module systems and to asynchronous programming.

Dan Rubin – «Seeing The Shot»

View on Eventbrite

Join Dan Rubin for a day of learning and exploration on the theory and practice of photography and editing, focusing on smartphone cameras. We’ll start with a review of the philosophy of shooting, discuss equipment, troubleshoot your personal photography problems and assess your own gear. Then we'll hit the streets, capturing images as we go. After this, we'll return to our desks to review the images we’ve shot, explore post-production techniques, and have general discussion and Q&A.

Denise Jacobs – « Hacking the Creative Brain»

View on Eventbrite

As tech industry professionals, what we need is a way to work better so that we can create more, right? Our brain is our most powerful tool, but so often the practices and culture of work only stifle our efforts to produce fresh ideas and approaches when most needed. In order to have more creative productivity, we need to hack how we use our brains on a daily basis in order to access more of its inherent processing and creative power.

Through exploring various concepts and approaches, including the neuroscience of creativity, productivity techniques, and emerging practices that spur innovation, we’ll discover not only the ways in which our brains work best, but also what’s behind the times when we feel on fire with creativity and when we don’t. We’ll translate this information into processes and techniques for dramatically enhanced creative productivity. Beware: this workshop challenges the standard norms around concentration, focus, productivity, and may change how you work… for the better.

Peter-Paul Koch – «Mobile Viewports»

View on Eventbrite

Is this workshop for you? To find out, try to understand the following sentence: "Once you set the layout viewport dimensions to the ideal viewport dimensions, you can use the device-width media query to read it out, and thereby get an idea what kind of device you're on."

a) If you understand exactly what this sentence says and spot the deliberate error I inserted, this workshop is not for you. I can't teach you anything.
b) If you run away screaming and definitely don't want to know, this workshop is not for you, either. Viewports are complicated
c) If you've got a vague idea this has something to do with responsive design, and you'd like to learn more, especially about that error, this workshop is for you.

In this one-day workshop PPK, who spent more time on the mobile viewport than anyone not working for a browser vendor, will treat the following topics:

How many viewports are there? (Spoiler: three.)
Why do we need so many? (Spoiler: Actually, for pretty good reasons.)
How do meta viewport and media queries work? (Spoiler: pretty well, as long as you keep it simple. But if you don't ... oh boy!)
And resolution? What is DPR anyway? (Spoiler: not what you think it is.)
Why does responsive design work? (Not how. Why.)

Also, you're encouraged to take real-world problems with you. We'll set aside some time to go through these problems, either in full session or one-on-one. You can't beat real-world problems when it comes to explaining complicated topics.

And devices. Please bring devices. The more obscure, the better.
